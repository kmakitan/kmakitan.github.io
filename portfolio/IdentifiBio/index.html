BACKGROUND

Problem Statement

The healthcare process is annoying. From paperwork to requesting appointments, it can be quite a hassle. We took this opportunity to alleviate the pains of a cumbersome experience.

MVP 1 UX/UI DESIGN

InVision Prototype (Medium-Fidelity)

For the first prototype, I made the experience simple by creating a fluid and recognizable flow.

MID-WAY CHALLENGES

Scope Change

After our first quarter, our project sponsor decided to change the project from a drug-testing to healthcare app. We quickly resolved this challenge by reviewing and making updates to our documents and diagrams with the stakeholder.

Uncooperative Developers

Although we initially agreed to build around the existing application, the outsource developers were uncooperative, refusing to share any documentation or API we could use. Since our team lacked experience with iOS development, this proved to be our greatest challenge.

MVP 2 UX/UI DESIGN

Origami Studio Prototype (High-Fidelity)

The core user experience flow remained mostly the same, and as I moved to higher fidelity mockups, the visual design came together. I prototyped in Origami Studio to better communicate the flow and slide animations of the experience to the engineers.

RESULT

By the end of the two quarters, our team was able to finish the project, meeting all the functional and non-functional requirements laid out in the beginning. Our project was also selected to receive the Ingenuity UCI Student Technology Showcase Award at the end of the year.

LEARNINGS

Team Cohesion

We met many difficult challenges throughout our project duration, but our will to cooperate, communicate, and achieve resolutions together enabled us to achieve the technology award at the end. Experiencing this team has helped me to understand the importance of creating the same strong cohesive dynamic in other future projects.

Design Feasibly

When I first created my designs, I did not take into account how difficult it would actually be to implement. I learned to cooperate more closely with engineers to create designs that were actually feasible buy the team.
