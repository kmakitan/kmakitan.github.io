User Research, Testing

ClimateLens

As part of an upper-division project course, my team created ClimateLens - an augmented reality mobile application which allows users to visualize the effects of climate change at their location. It also provides resources for local environmental organizations and pledges that users can commit to and keep track of.

My Role

UX Researcher

Duration

January 2018 - March 2018

Tools

Marvel, Invision, UsabilityHub.

Methods

Persona, Competitive Analysis, User Journey Map, ERAF Systems Diagram, Cognitive Walkthrough.

BACKGROUND

Problem Statement

Most people are aware and believe that global warming is a real issue. However, not many are actually taking action to do something about it. The problem is not lack of awareness anymore, but lack of urgency about climate change.

IDEATION

Approach

As part of the course, we followed a Lean UX methodology, and began by creating assumptions and ideating probable features and interfaces of our application.

Key Features

AR Experience

The main feature of our app will allow users to visualize the effect of climate change on local landmarks.

Community Building

There will be various community organizations users can join, as well as resources users can learn from.

Pledging

Users will be able to create pledges which will then allow them to track their progress.

USER RESEARCH

Paper Prototype

We quickly validated our approach by creating a paper prototype and testing it with users.

Research Output

I completed a competitive analysis, persona, user journey map, and ERAF systems diagram to help the rest of my team understand the user and product ecosystem, making it easier for them to design around the target user.

Core User Needs

Surreal Experience

Users need to have a surreal enough experience that will make them want to take action.

Take Action

Users need to be able to quickly take action with minimal effort.

Track Commitment

In order to keep up with any pledges users make, they need to be able to keep track of their progress.

TESTING

Cognitive Walkthrough

I conducted a cognitive walkthrough on the final MVP to assess the learnability of the application.

Task 1

Find out what the app is about.

Task 2

Simulate climate changes.

Task 3

See options for volunteering at a local organization.

Task 4

View my pledges.

I concluded that the app was friendly to new users, mostly because the app’s user experience is fairly linear and the onboarding experience helps users become familiar with the app.

FINAL MVP

LEARNINGS

Early Feedback

Following lean UX and testing our paper prototype early allowed us to validate our approach and continue in our direction. It’s important to fail earlier than later so that time and effort is not wasted on development.
